## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: Students are the largest user group in HEIs but often the least governed by security policies.
- **Context**: The "Bring Your Own Device" (BYOD) challenge in modern campus networks.

### 2. Theoretical Framework
- **Awareness Components**: Categorizing awareness into Knowledge, Attitude, and Behavior (P008).
- **Educational Intervention Theory**: How to design training that resonates with different student demographics.

### 3. Methodology
- **Target**: Undergraduate and postgraduate students across multiple faculties.
- **Instrument**: Validated Cybersecurity Awareness Scale (CAS).

### 4. Key Findings
- **Discovery 1**: Students in STEM fields have higher awareness than those in Arts/Humanities.
- **Discovery 2**: High usage of "Shadow IT" (P017) apps among students for collaboration.
- **Discovery 3**: Peer-led awareness campaigns are more effective than institutional ones for the student body.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Implications
- **Strategic Recommendation**: HEIs should implement faculty-specific security training modules to address the varying awareness baselines.
- **Relevance for Project 1**: This provides the final "Stakeholder" layer for the holistic readiness framework.
