## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Context**: HEIs handle massive amounts of sensitive student and research data.
- **Problem**: Security measures (MFA, monitoring) often conflict with academic privacy norms.

### 2. Theoretical Framework
- **Primary Theory**: The Privacy Paradox (valuing privacy but not acting to protect it, or resisting protection due to privacy fears).
- **Application**: Mapping how privacy concerns act as a "Brake" on readiness.

### 3. Methodology
- **Target**: Faculty, staff, and students across multiple universities.
- **Instrument**: Online survey measuring privacy concern levels and cybersecurity compliance intentions.

### 4. Key Findings
- **Discovery 1**: Significant negative correlation between privacy concerns and security tool adoption.
- **Discovery 2**: "Privacy Literacy" (knowledge of how data is used) reduces resistance to security tools.
- **Discovery 3**: Trust in the institution (P010) moderates the impact of privacy concerns.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Implications
- **Strategic Recommendation**: HEIs must be transparent about *why* security tools are needed and *how* they protect privacy, not just security.
- **Relevance for Project 1**: Supports the inclusion of **Privacy Value and Literacy** as critical diagnostic layers.
