## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Context**: The rising complexity of industrial decision support systems.
- **Gap**: Previous TAM models ignore the "Trust Mediator" in the context of AI-DSS.

### 2. Theoretical Background
- **Primary Theory**: Extended TAM.
- **Hypothesis**: Transparency -> Trust -> Perceived Usefulness -> Adoption.

### 3. Methodology
- **Design**: Cross-sectional quantitative survey.
- **Sample**: Professionals in technical sectors using AI-DSS.
- **Analysis Tool**: AMOS / PLS-SEM.

### 4. Results
- **Transparency Impact**: Significant impact on trust formation.
- **Mediating Role of Trust**: Trust acts as a full mediator between system quality and adoption intention.

### 5. Discussion & Conclusion
- **Strategic Implications**: To ensure adoption of AI in workplace systems (like the ones in HEIs), institutional transparency about *how the AI works* is mandatory.
