## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: Despite massive investments in security technology, the "Human Link" remains the weakest point due to policy non-compliance.
- **Objective**: Develop a comprehensive model that accounts for cognitive, motivational, and planned behavior factors.

### 2. Theoretical Framework Integration
- **OCT (Operant Conditioning)**: Focus on Rewards and Punishments.
- **PMT (Protection Motivation)**: Focus on Severity and Vulnerability.
- **TPB (Planned Behavior)**: Focus on Attitude, Subjective Norms, and Behavioral Control.

### 3. Methodology
- **Target**: Employees in various sectors.
- **Instrument**: 32-item survey validated via pilot study.
- **Analysis**: Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Modeling (PLS-SEM).

### 4. Results
- **Predictor 1: Attitude**: Strongest influence on intention.
- **Predictor 2: Perceived Behavioral Control**: High impact (if users feel they *can* comply, they intend to).
- **Predictor 3: Punishment**: Significant motivator (fear of consequences works in professional settings).
- **Surprise**: Subjective Norms (peer pressure) and Rewards (incentives) were **non-significant**.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Implications
- **Actionable Insight**: HEIs should focus less on "Gamification/Rewards" and more on "Usability/Control" and "Accountability/Punishment" to ensure readiness.
