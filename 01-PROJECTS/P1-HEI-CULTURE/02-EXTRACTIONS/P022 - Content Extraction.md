## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: HEIs mandate training, but breach rates remain high.
- **Objective**: Evaluate the impact of training beyond simple "Completion Rates."

### 2. The Kirkpatrick Evaluation Model
- **Level 1: Reaction**: Do staff like the training? (High in HEIs).
- **Level 2: Learning**: Do staff understand the concepts? (Moderate).
- **Level 3: Behavior**: Do staff change their security habits? (Low).
- **Level 4: Results**: Does the institution see fewer incidents? (Negligible correlation in traditional programs).

### 3. Methodology
- **Target**: Faculty and administrative staff at a large university.
- **Process**: Pre-training assessment, post-training quiz, and 3-month follow-up behavioral simulation.

### 4. Key Findings
- **Discovery 1**: "Knowledge" does not equal "Action." Staff pass quizzes but still click on simulated phishing links.
- **Discovery 2**: Faculty are more resistant to generic training than administrative staff.
- **Discovery 3**: "Contextualized" training (using academic examples) results in 50% higher behavioral retention.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Implications
- **Strategic Recommendation**: HEIs must move from "Annual Awareness" to "Just-in-Time" contextualized training (P015).
- **Relevance for Project 1**: Supports the inclusion of **Training Maturity and Relevancy** as critical diagnostic layers.
