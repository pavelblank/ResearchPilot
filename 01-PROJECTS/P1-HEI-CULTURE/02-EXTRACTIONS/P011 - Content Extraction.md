## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: Traditional trust models are dyadic (User-System). AI introduces a third actor: the Creator.
- **Question**: Does trust in the developer help or hinder trust in an autonomous system?

### 2. Theoretical Framework (The Triadic Model)
- **Actor 1: The User**: The trustor.
- **Actor 2: The Creator**: The entity responsible for the system's development.
- **Actor 3: The AI System**: The acting agent.
- **Mechanism**: Trust Transference (moving trust from known Creator to unknown AI).

### 3. Methodology
- **Approach**: Multi-scenario experiment.
- **Independent Variables**: AI Autonomy level and Creator Steerability.
- **Dependent Variable**: Trust Transference score.

### 4. Key Findings
- **Finding 1**: If the Creator can "steer" the AI, trust transfers easily.
- **Finding 2**: If the AI acts "autonomously," the user views it as a separate entity, breaking the trust link with the Creator.
- **Finding 3**: User-based steerability (the user being in control) actually *reduces* the need for creator trust.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Application
- **HEI Relevance**: When universities roll out mandatory security AI (e.g., threat detection), they must emphasize **Institutional Steerability** (that humans are still in control) to preserve the trust built between staff and the IT department.
