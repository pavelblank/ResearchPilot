## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: Higher Education Institutions (HEIs) are increasingly targeted by sophisticated cyber threats (phishing, ransomware).
- **Gap**: Lack of specific evaluations of AI models within the unique environment of campus networks.

### 2. Systematic Literature Review
- **Trend**: Shift from traditional, signature-based security to anomaly-based AI detection.
- **Context**: The role of "Small-Scale HEIs" and their specific vulnerability profiles.

### 3. Model Evaluation
- **Models Tested**: Random Forest, Support Vector Machine (SVM), Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN).
- **Metrics**: Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-Score.
- **Winner**: Random Forest for general threat detection; CNN for image-based/complex pattern recognition.

### 4. Incident Response Integration
- **Framework**: Automating the "Initial Triage" phase to support resource-constrained IT teams.
- **Insight**: AI reduces "Alert Fatigue," which is a primary cause of incident oversight in small universities.

### 5. Discussion & Application
- **HEI Strategic Advice**: Smaller institutions should prioritize **Random Forest** models for their computational efficiency and high precision in detecting anomalous campus traffic.
