## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Context**: UK universities are prime targets for cyber-attacks.
- **Problem**: Traditional security training fails to account for the "Cognitive Exhaustion" of academic staff.

### 2. Theoretical Framework
- **Primary Theory**: Cognitive Load Theory (CLT).
- **Application**: Security tasks are "Extraneous Load" that distracts from the "Germane Load" of research and teaching.

### 3. Methodology
- **Target**: Academic and research staff across 5 UK universities.
- **Instrument**: Fatigue Survey + Semi-structured interviews to capture "Workaround Stories."

### 4. Key Findings
- **Discovery 1**: 45% of participants regularly bypass security controls.
- **Discovery 2**: Fatigue levels are highest during peak teaching/grading periods.
- **Discovery 3**: "Shadow IT" (personal storage/apps) is the most common workaround.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Implications
- **Strategic Recommendation**: Security tools in HEIs must be designed for "Zero-Friction" to prevent the 45% workaround leak.
- **Relevance for Project 1**: Supports the inclusion of **Usability and Cognitive Load** as a critical diagnostic layer.
