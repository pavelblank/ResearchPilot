## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: Millions are spent on SETA with little evidence of actual behavioral improvement in employees.
- **Gap**: Lack of a standardized lifecycle model of critical success factors (CSFs).

### 2. Methodology (Three-Stage Inductive Study)
- **Stage 1**: 20 semi-structured interviews with SETA practitioners to identify emerging CSFs.
- **Stage 2**: Online survey (N=65) to rank and validate the importance of these CSFs.
- **Stage 3**: 9 follow-up interviews to refine the model and relationships.

### 3. The 11 Critical Success Factors (CSFs)
- **Design/Development**: Content relevance, Management Support, Alignment with Policy.
- **Implementation**: Interactive delivery, Multi-channel communication.
- **Evaluation**: Feedback loops, Behavioral tracking, Outcome metrics.

### 4. The Lifecycle Model
- **Logic**: CSFs are not static; they change in importance depending on the phase (Design vs. Implementation).
- **Core Finding**: Continuous evaluation is the "Glue" that holds the lifecycle together.

### 5. Discussion & Application
- **HEI Relevance**: For university readiness, this means SETA cannot be a "one-off" orientation module. It must be a continuous lifecycle integrated into the academic culture.
