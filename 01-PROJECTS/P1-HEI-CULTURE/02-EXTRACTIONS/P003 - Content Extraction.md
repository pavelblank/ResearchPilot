## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: Mandatory IS adoption often leads to trust decay if the system is difficult to use.
- **Goal**: Map the temporal stages of trust in professional environments.

### 2. Theoretical Framework
- **Model**: Dynamics of Trust in Information Systems.
- **Key Construct**: Trust Trajectories (how trust changes over time).

### 3. Methodology
- **Approach**: Quantitative longitudinal study.
- **Sample**: N=400 corporate and academic employees using new IS platforms.
- **Timeframe**: 3 waves over 12 months.

### 4. Findings
- **Discovery 1**: Initial trust is high but "fragile" (reputation-based).
- **Discovery 2**: System Reliability and Usability are the only significant predictors of "Stabilized Trust."
- **Discovery 3**: Poor usability leads to "Trust Erosion" which triggers behavioral workarounds (Shadow IT).

### 5. Discussion & Implications
- **Strategic Note**: For HEI readiness, this means if security tools (MFA, VPNs) are hard to use, users will lose trust in the institutional security mission.
