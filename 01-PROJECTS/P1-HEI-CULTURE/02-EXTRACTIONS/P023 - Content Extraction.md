## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: HEIs often have robust technical policies that fail to produce secure behaviors.
- **Objective**: Identify the socio-technical drivers of security compliance in universities.

### 2. Theoretical Framework
- **Socio-Technical Systems (STS)**: Viewing cybersecurity as an interaction between people, tasks, technology, and organizational structure.
- **Motivational Drivers**: Applying PMT to understand *why* staff choose to follow (or bypass) policies.

### 3. Methodology
- **Target**: Faculty and administrative staff at several HEIs.
- **Process**: Data-driven analysis of survey responses to map the relationships between Policy, Culture, and Behavior.

### 4. Key Findings
- **Discovery 1**: "Policy Clarity" (understanding the *what* and *how*) is more important than "Policy Stringency."
- **Discovery 2**: "Inclusive Governance" (where staff feel they have a voice) reduces the 45% workaround rate (P012).
- **Discovery 3**: Organizational Culture (P021) acts as a "Filter" for policy interpretation.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Implications
- **Strategic Recommendation**: HEI leaders must redesign their policies to be "Inclusive and Clear" rather than "Restrictive and Opaque."
- **Relevance for Project 1**: This provides the final "Governance-Human" link in the readiness framework.
