## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: Cybersecurity awareness training is often ignored or treated as a "tick-box" exercise.
- **Concept**: Fear appeals are common but often trigger reactance (rejection) rather than compliance.

### 2. Theoretical Development
- **The FRI Cue**: Proposing that threats to a "protected other" (family) are processed more deeply than self-threats.
- **ELM Integration**: FRI acts as a central cue prompting high elaboration.

### 3. Methodology
- **Participants**: Older adults (age 60+).
- **Intervention**: Two versions of a phishing awareness training (Individual threat frame vs. Family threat frame).
- **Analysis**: Comparison of behavioral intentions and elaboration scores.

### 4. Results
- **Main Effect**: The Family Risk frame led to significantly higher intention to process information and higher compliance with security advice.
- **Moderator**: Perceived self-efficacy was still required for the motivation to turn into action.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Application
- **HEI Relevance**: Staff in HEIs often have high "Institutional Fatigue." Personalizing the security mission (e.g., protecting student data as a "family responsibility") could be a superior motivator.
