## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Focus**: The high cost and complexity of BIA initiatives.
- **Problem**: Lack of depth in existing maturity models for ongoing evaluation.
- **Objective**: Develop a diagnostic framework (BIAED) that is easy to operationalize.

### 2. Related Work (Literature Review)
- **Analysis**: Review of 18 existing BIA maturity models.
- **Gap**: Models are often "one-size-fits-all" and lack input from diverse constituencies.

### 3. The BIAED Framework
- **Dimension 1: Technical Characteristics**: Data quality, metadata, tools, infrastructure.
- **Dimension 2: Facilitating Conditions**: Organizational culture, management support, alignment.
- **Dimension 3: Process Context**: Assessment, goal setting, strategy development, execution.

### 4. Methodology (Design Science)
- **Framework**: Follows Hevner’s 7 guidelines for Design Science research.
- **Process**: Iterative cycles of design, evaluation, and refinement.

### 5. Case Study Deployments
- **Case 1**: International Automobile Manufacturer (N=1050). Insights: Cultural misalignment between IT and business users.
- **Case 2**: Halifax Regional Municipality (N=300). Insights: Framework helped build a formal business case for BIA.
- **Case 3**: Property Valuation Services Corporation.

### 6. Discussion & Conclusion
- **Finding**: Multi-stakeholder perspectives are critical.
- **Implication**: Diagnostic evaluation must be a permanent part of the organizational lifecycle.
