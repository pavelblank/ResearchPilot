## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Context**: HEIs are high-value targets for both financially motivated and nation-state attackers.
- **Problem**: Technical defenses are bypassed by manipulating the human element.

### 2. The Social Engineering Landscape in HEIs
- **Techniques**: Phishing, Pre-texting, Baiting, and Tailgating in campus environments.
- **Vulnerability**: The "Open Research" culture vs. "Closed Security" requirements.

### 3. Methodology
- **Target**: Faculty, administrative staff, and research students.
- **Instrument**: Online survey measuring susceptibility to common social engineering scenarios.

### 4. Key Findings
- **Discovery 1**: Phishing remains the primary vector for initial compromise.
- **Discovery 2**: Grant notifications and collaboration requests are the most successful "Academic Triggers."
- **Discovery 3**: High frequency of attacks during peak academic periods (enrollment, finals).

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Implications
- **Strategic Recommendation**: HEIs must move beyond basic training to continuous, immersive phishing simulations tailored to academic workflows.
- **Relevance for Project 1**: Supports the inclusion of **Cultural Vulnerability and Awareness** as critical diagnostic layers.
