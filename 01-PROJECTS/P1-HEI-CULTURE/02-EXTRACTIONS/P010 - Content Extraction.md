## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: Most trust models assume adoption is voluntary. In security, adoption is mandatory.
- **Question**: How do users form trust when they *must* use the system?

### 2. Theoretical Framework
- **Key Construct: Regulatory Protection**: The perception that the institution has policies and controls to prevent harm to the user.
- **Key Construct: Mandatory Adoption**: The lack of behavioral alternatives in a professional setting.

### 3. Methodology
- **Approach**: Survey of 450+ professionals using mandatory enterprise systems.
- **Analysis**: PLS-SEM to test the weights of different trust drivers.

### 4. Findings
- **Result 1**: Regulatory Protection is the #1 driver of trust in mandatory settings.
- **Result 2**: Personal Disposition to Trust is less influential than Institutional Trust signals.
- **Result 3**: Trust mediates the relationship between institutional safeguards and actual system engagement.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Implications
- **HEI Relevance**: Universities must frame cybersecurity readiness as a **protective service for staff** (Regulatory Protection) to overcome the "academic freedom" barrier.
