## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: Enterprises spend billions on AI but see negligible productivity gains.
- **Question**: Why does superior technology fail to deliver superior outcomes?

### 2. Systematic Literature Review
- **Scope**: Multi-disciplinary review of management, IT, and psychology papers.
- **Gap Identified**: Lack of integration between "Technical Capability" and "Service Management Mindset."

### 3. The AI-Underperformance Paradox
- **Definition**: The disconnect between technical potential and actualized organizational value.
- **Cause**: Treating AI as a "plug-and-play" product rather than a lifecycle service.

### 4. Technological Servitization Framework
- **Pillar 1: Outcome-Oriented**: Focusing on what the AI *achieves* rather than what it *is*.
- **Pillar 2: Continuous Value**: Maintaining a long-term service relationship with AI agents.
- **Pillar 3: Cultural Alignment**: Preparing the workforce to interact with "AI-as-a-Service."

### 5. Conclusion
- **Implication for HEIs**: Universities must treat security not as a set of tools (MFA, firewalls) but as an ongoing **Security-as-a-Service** that requires cultural buy-in.
