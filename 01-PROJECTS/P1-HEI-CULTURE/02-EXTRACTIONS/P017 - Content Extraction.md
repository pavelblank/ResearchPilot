## 🔬 Section-by-Section Details

### 1. Introduction
- **Problem**: The COVID-19 pandemic permanently shifted HEIs toward hybrid/remote work, but security policies haven't caught up.
- **Objective**: Analyze the unique cybersecurity risks associated with remote academic work.

### 2. Theoretical Development
- **The Remote Risk Profile**: Categorizing risks into Technical (unsecured Wi-Fi), Behavioral (Shadow IT), and Environmental (home distractions).

### 3. Methodology
- **Target**: Faculty and staff at various international HEIs.
- **Instrument**: Online survey measuring security awareness, policy compliance, and remote work frequency.

### 4. Key Findings
- **Finding 1**: Remote staff are 30% more likely to use personal, unmanaged devices for sensitive work.
- **Finding 2**: Perceived "Policy Friction" (P012) is exacerbated by remote work, leading to higher workaround rates.
- **Finding 3**: Technical readiness (VPN, MFA) is high, but behavioral readiness (threat detection at home) is low.

### 5. Discussion & Strategic Implications
- **Actionable Insight**: HEIs must implement "Boundaryless" security training that addresses home environments and personal device security.
- **Relevance for Project 1**: Supports the inclusion of **Work Environment** as a primary diagnostic indicator.
