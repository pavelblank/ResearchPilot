## Knowledge Saved

### Paper: P001 - Foshay et al. (2015)

**Title**: A Comprehensive Diagnostic Framework for Evaluating Business Intelligence and Analytics Effectiveness

**Key Knowledge Points**:

1. **BIAED Framework** - Three-component approach for evaluating BIA effectiveness
2. **Assessment Dimensions** - Technical characteristics + Facilitating conditions
3. **Process Context** - Integration with strategy development
4. **Constituencies** - Users, Technical Team, Senior Management
5. **Data Quality** - User perception-based measurement
6. **Metadata** - Four essential types (definitional, quality, lineage, navigational)
7. **Culture** - Information-oriented culture is essential
8. **Iterative Process** - BIA is ongoing, never "done"
9. **Stakeholder Misalignment** - Common issue revealed by assessments
10. **Business Case Value** - Diagnostic toolkit essential for investment justification

**Citations Available**:
- Chen et al. (2012) - BIA definition
- Hevner et al. (2004) - Design science methodology
- Thamir & Theodoulidis (2013) - Maturity model classification
- Wang & Strong (1996) - Data quality dimensions
- And 20+ more references

**Methodology**:
- Design science paradigm
- 1500+ participants across 3 organizations
- Mixed methods (surveys + interviews)

**Limitations**:
- North American context only
- Self-reported data
- May need cultural adaptation

## How Your Research Brain Works

### When You Ask RA to Write
1. **Search**: RA checks KNOWLEDGE-BASE.md for relevant papers
2. **Connect**: RA looks at CONNECTIONS.md for related work
3. **Cite**: RA pulls from CITATIONS.md for proper referencing
4. **Insight**: RA uses insights/ for key points
5. **Verify**: RA only uses information from your papers (no hallucinations)

### When You Read New Papers
1. **Add**: New paper added to KNOWLEDGE-BASE.md
2. **Extract**: Key insights saved to insights/ folder
3. **Connect**: Relationships added to CONNECTIONS.md
4. **Cite**: Citations added to CITATIONS.md
5. **Update**: Statistics updated across all files

### When You Research
1. **Search**: By topic in KNOWLEDGE-BASE.md search index
2. **Find**: Relevant papers and concepts
3. **Connect**: Use CONNECTIONS.md to explore relationships
4. **Cite**: Use CITATIONS.md for proper attribution
